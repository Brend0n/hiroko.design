THE CHALLENGE

Big frustration with the gesture based control

As you can see in the video above, the mirror is designed to control by gestures. After our first big user test on the beginning of 2023, we quickly ran into a challenging (but valuable) insight: users were frustrated by the gesture controls. The problems came from both technical limitations and UX difficulty.
The AI had trouble consistently recognizing gestures, which led to moments like:
"Wait‚Äîwhy did it just switch widgets? I didn‚Äôt ask for that!"
And memorability was another issue:
"Umm‚Ä¶ what was the gesture to close the tools again?"
UX SOLUTION #1

Finding the Right Set of Gestures

We needed gestures that were simple for users and easy for the AI to recognize. Through extensive testing and data analysis, we narrowed it down to just four core gestures that offered a great balance of usability and technical accuracy.
UX SOLUTION #2

Real-Time Interactive Tutorial

Since this was a new way to interact, I designed an always-accessible tutorial with live camera feedback, so users could practice at any time. After just a few tries, most people felt confident and comfortable using gestures.
UX SOLUTION #2.5

Adding the Gesture Guide

To reduce false activations, we limited gesture recognition to specific conditions. I designed a gesture guide that appeared on the screen whenever gesture recognition was activated. It served two important roles: letting users know the system was ready to receive commands, and reminding them of the available gestures in that context. Simple, visual, and super helpful.
UX SOLUTION #3

Simplified Navigation

We focused the interaction design around our small set of gestures. I hosted workshops with engineers and the PM to ensure we aligned with both user expectations and technical constraints. The goal was to make it feel effortless.
UX SOLUTION #4

Streamlined Information Architecture

The mirror is meant to enhance daily life, not slow it down. I removed non-essential (admin) features from the mirror interface, designed a companion smartphone app for tasks like account setup and widget customization. Thanks to the Baracoda design system, the design to imprementation took only 3 sprints.
UX SOLUTION #5

Cleaner Mirror UI

Mirror reflections can make reading on-screen text surprisingly tricky. Through iterative testing, I optimized font size, contrast, and layout. I also reduced visual clutter by minimizing how much info appears on one screen‚Äîkeeping things clear, simple, and fast to digest.

External User Test

After months of iterations and refinement, we did another round of external testing, and the results were fantastic: +90% of users gave positive feedback on both gesture control and overall usability.
