We needed a set of gestures that would strike a balance between ease of use and high recognition accuracy by the AI. After conducting a large amount of user testing and data collection, we selected 4 primary gestures.

Preventing False Positives

Avoiding false positives‚Äîunintended gesture activations during everyday bathroom use-was another big challenge. After extensive user testing, we developed a gesture recognition system that activates only under specific conditions.
To support this, I designed
  • A visual indicator to signal when gesture recognition is active.
  • A gesture guide that appears on-screen when the system is ready, providing real-time feedback.

Reorganise the information architecture

To ensure seamless control with minimal gestures, I restructured the information architecture. Since the mirror is meant to enhance, not complicate, daily routines, I
  • Removed most administrative features from the mirror itself.
  • Developed a companion smartphone app for tasks like account setup and widget customization.
