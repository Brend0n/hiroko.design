Designing the Future of Touchless AI

Project Name
BMirror
Context
A world-first AI-driven smart mirror designed to enhance mental wellness through interactive, touchless technology.
The Goal
Develop a seamless, gesture-based interface that integrates advanced AI (computer vision) into a user's daily bathroom routine without the need for physical contact.
My Role
Solo Product Designer. I led the end-to-end UX/UI, working in close collaboration with AI engineers, hardware/software teams, and product marketing to translate technical AI capabilities into an intuitive user experience.
The Result
Awarded the CES Innovation Award 2024🏆. Through rigorous iterative testing and refinement, we successfully increased the task success rate from 0% to 90%+ within 8 months.
DISCOVERY

Solving the "Intuition vs. Technical Constraint" Dilemma

Initial user testing with the first prototype in early 2023 revealed a critical failure: The task success rate was 0%. There was massive frustration with gesture control due to technical constraints (the AI struggling to recognize intended gestures) and high cognitive overload.

The Challenge

  • Unpredictability & Fear: The AI struggled to distinguish between intended gestures and natural movements (like fixing hair). This caused frequent unintended commands, making users feel "scared" and out of control of their own mirror.
  • Mental Model Conflict: To satisfy engineering requirements for distinct "data points," we initially used 8 complex gestures. However, these lacked "real-world physics"—for example, requiring a "Victory sign" to navigate down instead of a natural "point down" motion.
  • The Memorability Gap: Users could not recall the specific 8-gesture library or which movement triggered which action, rendering the "invisible" interface practically unusable.
  • Visibility Barriers: Reflections and varied bathroom lighting made digital content difficult to read, further breaking the feedback loop between the user and the AI.

Core Insight

The key to success was finding the equilibrium between Technical Feasibility (what the AI can see) and Human Intuity (what a human expects). We realized that for a touchless system to be trusted, it must provide Immediate Visual Affordance—bridging the gap by making the machine's "understanding" visible to the user in real-time.
UX SOLUTION #1

Reducing Cognitive Load

To address the "Memorability Gap", I overhauled the interaction architecture:
  • The "5-Gesture" Constraint: Through iterative user testing and data collection, I successfully reduced the library from 8 gestures to just 5 high-accuracy, physics-based movements.
  • Intuitive Mapping: I re-mapped controls to follow real-world expectations (e.g., pointing direction to move content, thumb up to confirm/OK), ensuring interaction felt like an extension of natural movement.
UX SOLUTION #2

On-Demand Mastery & Visual Aid

To solve the "Unpredictability" and "Fear" found in testing, I moved away from "Black Box" interaction:
  • Live Practice Mode: I designed a dedicated mode accessible at any time where users can practice gestures with a live camera feed. This shows them exactly how the AI "sees" them, allowing for instant self-correction and mastery.
  • Persistent Visual Hints: I implemented a non-intrusive "Gesture Guide" that appears when user presence is detected, providing a subtle visual reminder of the 5 core movements.
UX SOLUTION #3

Hardware-Specific UI Optimization

Knowing that standard UI fails on a reflective surface, I optimized the visual language for the glass medium:
  • Contrast & Legibility: I ran iterative tests on the physical hardware to find the exact high-contrast color values and font scales that remained legible against complex reflections and varying light.
  • Strategic Multi-Device Off-loading: To ensure the mirror remained a calming wellness tool, I off-loaded all complex configuration and deep-data analysis to a companion mobile app. This allowed the mirror UI to focus exclusively on "at-a-glance" bathroom utility and real-time interaction.
  • Progressive Information Disclosure: I implemented a minimalist architecture that prioritizes a single focus point. By limiting active UI elements, I minimized visual noise, ensuring the user could still use the mirror for its literal purpose while receiving digital insights.
UX SOLUTION #4

Cross-Functional Design Synthesis

I acted as the "UX Glue" to orchestrate fragmented technical teams into a single, cohesive product:
  • AI Feasibility: Collaborated with AI engineers to train models to ignore "natural noise" (like brushing teeth) while remaining responsive to the 5 core gestures.
  • Engineering Alignment: Coordinated with hardware and software teams to ensure mirror luminosity and data flow to the mobile companion app were instantaneous, creating a seamless multi-device experience.
