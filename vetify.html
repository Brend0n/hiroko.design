AI-Assisted Consultation Platform for Veterinarians

Project Name
VetfyPro
Context
A strategic B2B2B partnership with a leading UAE pharmaceutical company to develop an AI-driven SaaS veterinary platform designed to optimize clinical efficiency and data synthesis during consultations.
The Goal
Centralize fragmented patient data and utilize a specialized AI model to suggest differential diagnoses and treatment plans while keeping the veterinarian in total control.
My Role
Solo Product Designer (Concept to MVP Launch). I was responsible for the end-to-end product lifecycle, including ethnographic research, product strategy, UX/UI design, and stakeholder management.
The Result
Launched from concept to market in 6 months; currently used by 200+ clinics daily.
DISCOVERY

Bridging the Trust Gap

Utilizing in-clinic shadowing and contextual inquiry, I analyzed the high-pressure environment of veterinary consultations to identify how AI could seamlessly augment the clinician's workflow without introducing friction or slowing down the process.

The Challenge

  • Cognitive Overload: Fragmented data (histories, labs, notes, owner input) across multiple legacy tools leads to diagnostic stress.
  • The Trust Barrier: There is deep skepticism regarding "Black Box" AI making autonomous decisions about animal health.
  • Physical Constraints: Vets are handling animals and communicating with owners; they have zero bandwidth for complex PC interactions.

Core Insight

AI adoption in medicine depends on Clinical Authority. Vets don't want the AI to "do the work" or make final calls—they want it to "surface the proof" so they can remain the ultimate decision-maker, supported by data-driven evidence.
UX SOLUTION: Human-Centered AI Integration #1

Clinical Authority & AI Explainability

To ensure the vet remained the primary authority, I designed a "Human-in-the-Loop" interaction pattern focused on transparency:
  • Vet-Led Data Control : The AI is strictly forbidden from generating or modifying any medical information without explicit authorization. It functions as a medical assistant, not an autonomous agent.
  • Human Authorization Loop : Instead of auto-updating, I implemented a non-intrusive notification: "New information detected. Would you like to regenerate the recommendation?" This keeps the vet in charge of the system's logic.
  • Source Transparency Vets can click any AI-generated suggestion to see the "Backed Proof"—highlighting exactly which previous lab result or history note the AI referred to, allowing for instant verification.
UX SOLUTION: Human-Centered AI Integration #2

Conversational Support Layer

I integrated an LLM-powered chatbot to move beyond static UI forms:
  • Natural Language Queries : Vets can ask complex, environmental-specific questions like "This medication is out of stock in the UAE, suggest a localized alternative."
  • The Result : Transitioned the AI from a technical utility to a collaborative, supportive partner that respects the vet's professional expertise.
UX SOLUTION #3

AI-generated Recommendation and chatbot support

We designed the AI experience to enhance the vet’s authority while ensuring full transparency and control. When new data is added, the system notifies the vet that an updated recommendation is available, but it never changes automatically. This approach prevents confusion and builds trust — vets always know when and why the AI’s suggestions have changed. Through a chatbot interface, vets can explore alternative options, ask for clarifications, or regenerate recommendations on demand. This conversational layer made interacting with AI feel natural, supportive, and consistent with the veterinarian’s decision-making flow
UX SOLUTION #4

Report & Pet Owner Summary

The final report is AI-pre-filled for quick editing, but vets can freely write and finalize their decisions. We also added a friendly summary email for pet owners, clearly explaining the consultation, diagnosis, and medication to enhance engagement and adherence.

Outcome

We launched the MVP in July 2025 with +150 clinics on board. Early feedback was very positive — vets appreciated the flexibility of the consultation flow and the supportive, non-intrusive role of the AI, that was a relief.

Reflection

This project was a fast-paced, collaborative journey — from idea to launch in just 6 months. It reinforced one key lesson: good AI design starts with trust and empathy. When technology supports rather than replaces professionals, it truly becomes meaningful.
