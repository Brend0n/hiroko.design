AI-Assisted Consultation Platform for Veterinarians

Project: VetfyPro
Type: B2B2B SaaS web application
Client: UAE-based pharmaceutical company
Role: Lead Product Designer
Our client wanted to create an innovative digital service to help veterinary clinics work more efficiently during consultations. The goal was to centralize patient data and use an AI model trained for veterinary contexts to suggest differentials, diagnostic tests, and treatment plans — while keeping the veterinarian fully in control. As the Lead Product Designer, I guided the process from concept to MVP launch, focusing on making the AI experience intuitive, efficient, and trustworthy. It was also a great chance to explore how AI can support, not replace, professionals.
THE CHALLENGE

Vets Face Information Overload

Veterinarians deal with fragmented systems and scattered data — patient histories, lab results, and owner notes often exist across multiple tools. During a busy consultation, it’s difficult to retrieve, synthesize, and act on all this information efficiently. This complexity creates cognitive overload, stress, and occasionally, diagnostic errors.
Research & Key Insights

Intuitive interface with trustworthy AI experience

Through interviews with vets and clinic staff, we identified some critical insights:
  • Many vets are not very tech-savvy and prefer simple, familiar interfaces.
  • They are skeptical of AI and want to remain the final decision-maker for themselves.
  • Consultations aren’t linear — New information, such as lab results, often appears mid-session, requiring flexibility rather than a fixed step-by-step process.
UX SOLUTION #1

Defining the MVP

The client initially envisioned a highly feature-rich platform. My first task was to prioritize the MVP based on:
  • Desirability — via user interviews
  • Viability — in a workshop with the client
  • Feasibility — in collaboration with engineers
This alignment helped us clarify the MVP’s purpose and focus on creating meaningful AI-human collaboration, rather than feature overload.
UX SOLUTION #2

Flexible Consultation Flow

Instead of a rigid, linear process, we introduced sidebar navigation, allowing vets to move freely between sections as new data appeared.
UX SOLUTION #3

AI-generated Recommendation and chatbot support

We designed the AI experience to enhance the vet’s authority while ensuring full transparency and control. When new data is added, the system notifies the vet that an updated recommendation is available, but it never changes automatically. This approach prevents confusion and builds trust — vets always know when and why the AI’s suggestions have changed. Through a chatbot interface, vets can explore alternative options, ask for clarifications, or regenerate recommendations on demand. This conversational layer made interacting with AI feel natural, supportive, and consistent with the veterinarian’s decision-making flow
UX SOLUTION #4

Report & Pet Owner Summary

The final report is AI-pre-filled for quick editing, but vets can freely write and finalize their decisions. We also added a friendly summary email for pet owners, clearly explaining the consultation, diagnosis, and medication to enhance engagement and adherence.

Outcome

We launched the MVP in July 2025 with +150 clinics on board. Early feedback was very positive — vets appreciated the flexibility of the consultation flow and the supportive, non-intrusive role of the AI, that was a relief.

Reflection

This project was a fast-paced, collaborative journey — from idea to launch in just 6 months. It reinforced one key lesson: good AI design starts with trust and empathy. When technology supports rather than replaces professionals, it truly becomes meaningful.
